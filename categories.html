The three pillars of well-being

The action of our organization is focused on the three pillars of the Well-Being:

  • Health
  • Social Connections
  • Financial Stability

Read below how we categorize these major sectors in order to identify and address the problem with the best way possible.

Health

Physical Health: Involves maintaining a healthy body through regular exercise, proper nutrition, sufficient sleep, and routine medical check-ups. .

Mental Health: Encompasses emotional and psychological well-being. It involves managing stress, maintaining a positive outlook, and seeking help when dealing with mental health issues such as depression or anxiety.

Overall Wellness: Integrates physical and mental health to ensure a balanced and holistic approach to well-being. This can include practices such as mindfulness, meditation, and engaging in activities that promote relaxation and joy.

Social Connections

Relationships: Having strong, positive relationships with family, friends, and partners is crucial for emotional support and a sense of belonging.

Community Engagement: Being involved in community activities or groups can provide a sense of purpose and connection to others. This could be through volunteering, participating in clubs, or being part of local organizations.

Social Support: Access to a network of people who can provide practical and emotional support during times of need is essential. This support system can improve resilience and coping abilities during difficult times.

Financial Stability

Income and Employment: Having a stable and sufficient income to meet basic needs such as housing, food, healthcare, and education is fundamental. Employment also contributes to a sense of purpose and identity.

Savings and Investments: Building savings and making prudent investments can provide financial security and peace of mind, ensuring that there are resources available for emergencies and future needs.

Debt Management: Effectively managing and minimizing debt is important to avoid financial stress and maintain financial health. This involves budgeting, planning, and making informed financial decisions.
